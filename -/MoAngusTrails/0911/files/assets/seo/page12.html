Missouri Angus News
by Brent Murphy, President
I hope this fnds everyone safe and looking forward to a productive
fall. Summer is about over and we are off to fall production sales,
weaning and another calving season. I wish everyone well with those
activities and events.
The Missouri State Fair is behind us and I want to congratulate
all the exhibitors and people involved in making all events a success.
Everyone who participated is a winner! I noticed several of our junior
members being recognized asYouth in Ag Scholarship recipients and
the association should be proud of our strong junior program. I think
the best arena for our youth is the junior livestock show and programs
associated with the livestock industry. Sometimes we are quick to
look at all the bad in the world and we don’t take enough time to
realize all the good. I challenge everyone to look at the positives
we have around us and build from that and let’s not dwell on the less
desirable!
Missouri will be hosting the National Junior Show in 2013 in
Kansas City. Now is a great time to volunteer to help make this a
great event.
It is also time to start thinking about your consignments for the
annual Missouri Angus Breeders’ Futurity scheduled for February
25-26, 2012. It is never too early to start on those cattle so we can
provide the best offering possible.
The Missouri Angus Advantage Plus Sale will be held on
December 2
nd
at the Marshall LivestockAuction. If you are interested
in consigning to this event please contact Josh Worthington or any
board member. The current cattle market will be favorable for a great
sale!
I want to remind all committee members that committee reports
will be due at the November 20th board of directors meeting. I
encourage everyone to participate in your committee meetings and
share ideas that will develop programs for the continuation of a
strong Missouri Angus Association.
Until next month, good luck and please don’t hesitate to give me
a call if I can help you in anyway!
The Historic Angus Herd Award is presented to Angus breed-
ers or immediate family who have been in continuous production of
registered Angus cattle for 50 years or more. The American Angus
Association
®
is honored to recognize the Elbert Angus Farm, Pierce
City, Mo., as a Historic Angus Herd.
The ranch began when Kenneth Elbert bought his frst Angus
cattle in 1959 and joined the Angus Association in 1960. Cattle were
purchased from Zink Angus Farm, Appleton City, Mo.; Keith Nu,
Republic, Mo.; and Level Acres, Pierce City, Mo.
Elbert purchased Black Revolution 850 in 1970. The bull went
on to win Grand Champion at the 1971 Ozark Empire Fair, Spring-
feld, Mo., and the Kansas State Fair in Hutchinson, Kan.
The herd of 250 registered Angus females is still owned and
operated by founder Elbert and his son, David. The females in the
program have been retained for the past 50-plus consecutive years,
which allows Elbert Angus Farm to merchandise their cattle through
private treaty. Recently, the farm gained 15 Pathfnder cows. The fa-
ther and son pair is also involved in the Angus Association’s Angus
Herd Improvement Records (AHIR
®
) program.
For more information about the Historic Angus Herd Award,
Angus cattle and the American Angus Association’s programs and
services, visit www.angus.org.
The American Angus Association is the nation’s largest beef
organization, serving 30,000 members across the United States and
Canada. It provides programs and services to farmers, ranchers and
others who rely on the power of Angus to produce quality genetics for
the beef industry and quality beef for consumers.
