Auxiliary News
by Susan Coon, President
Greetings! Soon, we will be delivering Kathryn to Mizzou and
Jacob will begin his junior year in high school. Summer has certainly
passed by quickly. Hopefully this summer everyone had great expe-
riences, made lots of memories showing cattle and met lots of new
friends.
On the ranch, we have a few fall calves already on the ground
and are watching the others awaiting them to drop a new baby. The
kids are watching their past show heifers especially close, hoping
they get an outstanding prospect for next show season. Seasons seem
to come and go and we continue to be excited about the opportunities
the next season offers. 
This fall the auxiliary team will plan and organize the work-
shops we will offer Friday evening at the Futurity. These workshops
are intended to assist the youth with the different contest teams at the
National Junior Angus Show and offer them guidance in developing
their ideas.
If you are interested in assisting us with the workshops please
drop me an email at scoon@atsu.edu. 
Angus breeders since 1964, Curtis and Ann Long, Butler, MO,
continue to back their breed and the kids that show them. For the
13th consecutive year, Briarwood Angus Farms challenged FFA and
4-H youth to breed, feed and lead Angus to the winner’s circle at the
2011 Missouri State Fair Carcass Contest. Cash awards and show
halters went to six top individuals.
Tanner Drechsel, Boonville, exhibited the FFA Grand Champion
Angus Market Steer with Abby Sudbrock of Centralia FFA taking
Reserve in the FFA division.
In the 4-H category Tevin Taylor of Bates County was named
Grand Champion and Nicolas Rhodes of Linn County took home the
Reserve Champion Live Angus Carcass Steer title.
Kendra Pryor, Pleasant Hill FFA won Grand Champion Angus
Carcass and Hannah Moyer, Lamar FFA received Reserve Grand
Champion for her Angus carcass.
Briarwood Angus Farms strives to encourage the next genera-
tion of Angus breeders to focus on the end product. Dr. Long and
the entire crew at Briarwood stand behind their motto “Your Carcass
Connection” because they believe in a steadfast commitment to the
longstanding reputation of Angus genetics. BWF carcasses average
15” REA and 90% Choice as a result of intensive selection for feed
effcient and productive stock. Many young producers annually at-
tend Briarwood Farms spring Open House Private Treaty Sale to
build their own Angus herds with such proven genetics.
Each year, more and more young winners of the MSF Angus
Carcass Contest beneft from the generosity of Dr. Curtis and Ann
Long. And the Angus industry benefts as well knowing that young,
progressive producers are taking Angus the right direction.
The 2011 winners will be acknowledged at the Annual Missouri
Angus Futurity Banquet where they will receive their prize money
during an awards presentation. For more information about the an-
nual State Fair Contest, visit http://www.mostatefair.com or http://
briarwoodangusfarms.com orr contact Briarwood Farms at 660-679-
3459. Pre-register for next year’s contest and be sure to check entry
requirements in advance. And to learn more about the Missouri Ju-
nior Angus program, go to http://missouriangus.org/juniors.html
